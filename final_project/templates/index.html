The Dice Study

A study on valuations of economic decisions

Study Objective:

A behavioral intervention to nudge people towards maximizing the expected value of economic decisions instead of relying on suboptimal heuristics.

The Experiment:

Participants played a dice rolling game in which they guessed (or bet) what number they thought might be the outcome of a single die roll (i.e., 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, or 6). After guessing a number, they rolled the die. If their guess and the number they rolled were the same (e.g., they guessed 4 and rolled 4), they earned that amount in quarters (e.g., 4 quarters). If they did not correctly guess the number, they won nothing.

The data:

182 participants played the dice rolling game for a total of 10 rounds. The independent variables are the framing of the game (i.e., the game was presented as a either a potential loss or an uncertain gain) and the salience of the quarters they used to play (i.e., if the quarters were salient, or handed to them directly after winning, versus electronic quarters allocated on computer).
